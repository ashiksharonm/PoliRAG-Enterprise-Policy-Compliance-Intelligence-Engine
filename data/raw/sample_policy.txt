ACME Corporation — Data Retention & Security Policy
Document ID: POL-2025-001
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Classification: Internal

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes requirements for the retention, protection, and
disposal of company records and data in compliance with applicable federal
and state regulations, including SOX, HIPAA, and GDPR where applicable.

2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, temporary workers, and
third-party service providers who create, receive, maintain, or manage
company records in any format — paper, electronic, or otherwise.

3. RETENTION PERIODS

3.1 Financial Records
All financial records including general ledgers, accounts payable and
receivable, tax filings, and audit reports must be retained for a minimum
of seven (7) years from the end of the fiscal year in which they were
created.

3.2 Employee Records
Human resources records must be retained for the duration of employment
plus five (5) years following separation. This includes offer letters,
performance reviews, disciplinary actions, and benefits enrollment.

3.3 Customer Data
Customer personally identifiable information (PII) must be retained for
the duration of the business relationship plus three (3) years, unless a
longer period is required by contractual or regulatory obligations.

3.4 Email Communications
Business-related email communications must be retained for three (3) years.
Emails subject to legal hold must be retained indefinitely until the hold
is released by the Legal department.

4. DATA SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

4.1 Password Policy
All system passwords must meet the following minimum requirements:
- Minimum length: 12 characters
- Must contain at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter,
  one digit, and one special character
- Passwords must be changed every 90 days
- Previous 12 passwords may not be reused

4.2 Encryption
All data classified as Confidential or Restricted must be encrypted both
at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher). Encryption keys
must be rotated annually.

4.3 Access Control
Access to sensitive data must follow the principle of least privilege.
All access requests must be approved by the data owner and reviewed
quarterly. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for access to
all production systems.

5. INCIDENT RESPONSE

5.1 Reporting
Any suspected data breach or security incident must be reported to the
Information Security team within one (1) hour of discovery via the
incident hotline or the security@acme.example.com email address.

5.2 Escalation Procedure
- Severity 1 (Critical): Immediate notification to CISO, Legal, and
  executive leadership. External notification to affected parties within
  72 hours as required by GDPR.
- Severity 2 (High): Notification to CISO within 4 hours. Investigation
  initiated within 8 hours.
- Severity 3 (Medium): Notification to Security Operations within 24
  hours. Standard investigation timeline.

6. DISPOSAL

Records that have exceeded their retention period and are not subject to
any legal hold must be securely destroyed. Paper records shall be
cross-cut shredded. Electronic records shall be permanently deleted using
NIST 800-88 compliant sanitization methods.

7. COMPLIANCE VIOLATIONS

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and
including termination of employment. Employees must report suspected
violations to the Compliance department or through the anonymous
compliance hotline.

8. REVIEW

This policy will be reviewed and updated annually by the Chief Compliance
Officer in consultation with Legal, Information Security, and Human
Resources departments.
